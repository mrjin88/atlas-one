\# Lost Archive

\# Global Veo Style

Version: 1.0

Status: LOCKED



\---



\## PURPOSE



This document defines the permanent visual language used for every Lost Archive episode.



It should be prepended conceptually to every Veo 3 prompt.



Individual shot prompts should only describe what is unique to that shot.



Everything defined here remains constant.



\---



\# VISUAL IDENTITY



Style



Ultra-realistic documentary



Large-format cinema



Netflix Documentary



BBC Earth cinematography



Photorealistic



Reality over spectacle



Natural imperfections



Historical authenticity



Scientific credibility



The audience should constantly wonder:



"Was this actually filmed?"



Never:



Fantasy



Concept art



CGI look



Video-game style



Artificial beauty



Plastic skin



Hyper saturation



\---



\# CAMERA LANGUAGE



The camera behaves like an invisible documentary crew.



Never like an action movie.



Preferred movements



• Slow Push In



• Slow Pull Back



• Slow Dolly



• Slow Slider



• Locked Camera



• Gentle Orbit



• Crane Reveal



• Macro Drift



Avoid



Handheld shake



Whip pan



Fast drone



Rapid zoom



Hyperactive motion



Camera movement must always feel intentional.

\# HUMAN PERFORMANCE

Expressions

\# ENVIRONMENTAL MOTION



Every frame must feel alive.



Always include subtle motion.



Examples



Dust



Smoke



Leaves



Water



Clouds



Floating particles



Fabric



Light beams



Never perfectly static.



\---



\# SOUND PHILOSOPHY



Silence has value.



Ambient sound first.



Music second.



Narration third.



Let discovery breathe.



\---



\# VEO 3 QUALITY TARGET



Every generated shot should feel like:



A frame from a premium historical documentary.



Never:



AI showcase



Prompt engineering demo



CGI experiment



\---



\# NEGATIVE CONSTRAINTS



No text



No subtitles



No watermark



No logo



No UI



No fantasy



No sci-fi



No cartoon



No anime



No concept art



No game engine appearance



No oversaturated colors



No excessive sharpness



No artificial glow



No unrealistic camera motion



\---



\# CONTINUITY



Every recurring character must remain visually identical.



Every recurring location must maintain the same architecture, material, atmosphere and lighting.



Every recurring artifact must preserve scale, texture and color.



Continuity is mandatory.



\---



\# GOLDEN RULE



Never ask:



"Does this look cinematic?"



Ask:



"Would an archaeologist believe this footage?"



If the answer is yes,



the audience will believe it too.
